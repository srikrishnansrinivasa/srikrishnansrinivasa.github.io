About

Over 20 years, I've built and scaled healthcare operations that serve some of the largest health plans in the U.S. — combining deep domain expertise with an AI-first mindset to deliver results that actually move the needle.

My career didn't follow a neat path. I started processing claims on the floor, learned how health plans actually work from the inside, and spent years building delivery operations across India and the Philippines before taking on P&L leadership for a $500M+ BPaaS business. Every role taught me something the next one needed.

At Cognizant, I built one of the largest non-captive clinical services organizations globally — growing from zero to 3,000+ credentialed clinicians in the Philippines and securing offshore URAC accreditation, one of the first providers in the world to do so. I then led the acquisition and full operational takeover of TMG Health — a "health plan in a box" acquired from HCSC — and ran it as a going concern while integrating it into a broader BPaaS vision.

At Firstsource, I grew the health plan portfolio from 12 to 50+ clients at 25%+ CAGR, built new practices in Risk Adjustment, Medicaid Redetermination, and Digital Health, and deployed AI-enabled workflows delivering $10M+ in annual savings — while setting a new company performance record.

Today, as SVP Strategic Accounts & Growth, I work at the C-suite interface with America's largest health plans — not just to serve them, but to be their partner in what comes next.

How I Think & Work

01
AI-First. Always.
I don't bolt AI onto existing processes — I start by asking whether the process should exist at all. My 3P framework — Prevent, Process, Predict — eliminates waste before automating it. Every engagement starts with one question: what would this look like if we built it from scratch today?
02
Operations as Strategy
The best strategy insights I've found are in floor walks, SLA reviews, and root-cause analyses. I bring P&L discipline into every client conversation — because outcomes that can't be measured can't be sustained. I'm not a consultant who leaves. I'm an operator who stays.
03
Build the Team First
Every organization I've built — from Chennai to Manila to Dallas — started with the same question: who do we need, and how do we make them excellent? Hiring, training, and culture are not HR functions. They are the product. Get the team right, and the outcomes follow.
04
Domain Depth as Moat
Healthcare BPS is not a commodity market if you actually understand it. CMS regulatory shifts, NCQA standards, UM criteria, Stars methodology — these aren't compliance checkboxes. They're the language my clients think in. I meet them there, and that changes the conversation.
05
ROI Before Appetite
New tools earn their seat at the table by delivering on their promises — not by being interesting. I require demonstrated ROI before approving incremental spend. This is what makes transformation durable instead of decorative.
06
Long Relationships. Not Short Wins.
Eight of the ten largest health plans in America. Eighteen of thirty-two Blue Plans. These aren't transaction records — they're relationships built over years of showing up, delivering, and being honest when things get hard. That's the only kind of growth that compounds.

"Can AI Help Me Draw Better?" — My Daughter's Simple Questions About a Complex Future

Explaining AI to a 9-year-old is the ultimate clarity test. Last weekend, my daughter put me to the challenge: "Is AI like a robot? Can it read my mind? Will it help me draw better? Can AI make me a better cook to become Chef one day?"

What followed was a conversation that reminded me — big ideas don't need big words. The best way to understand AI? Through everyday moments. We compared it to her favorite voice assistant, how Netflix recommends shows, how maps predict traffic. AI learns from patterns — kind of like how she knows I'll always say "yes" to ice cream.

She worried AI might "take over." We laughed about how even the smartest app can't replace her giggles, messy art, or curiosity. AI is just a tool — we decide how to use it. Kids today are growing up alongside AI. Helping them see it as a teammate, not a threat, empowers them to imagine what's possible.
